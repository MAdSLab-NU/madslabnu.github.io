About

I am a graduate student in the Department of Chemistry and Chemical Biology, where I spend my days investigating the molecular mechanisms by which natural products, such as bacteriocins, target and kill Gram-negative bacteria. My goal is to decode nature's antimicrobial warfare and develop strategies to engineer next-generation therapeutics.

I grew up in the vibrant city of Islamabad, Pakistan, and then moved to Lahore, where my love for multidisciplinary learning first took root. I've been fortunate to explore diverse academic realms through my studies in social sciences, arts, and physical sciences, earning several master's degrees along the way. This eclectic approach has become my greatest asset, allowing me to tackle complex scientific challenges from multiple perspectives.

When I'm not busy convincing stubborn bacteria to surrender their secrets in the lab, you might find me celebrating one of Boston's simple pleasures: rain, savoring the petrichor that soil bacteria release, nature's reminder that not all microbes are adversaries.

My belief about life and learning resonates deeply with the words of a fellow Renaissance spirit: "To develop a complete mind: Study the science of art; Study the art of science. Learn how to see. Realize that everything connects to everything else." —Leonardo da Vinci".
